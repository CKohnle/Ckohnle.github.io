Acoustics / Experimental Engineering Independent Project

Sound Attenuation by Transmission Yielding Resonators (SATYR)

An experimental engineering project exploring whether collimated acoustic waves can be used to reduce acoustic-range sound through controlled interference in the propagation medium.

Principal Investigator

Christian Kohnle

Collaborators

Shahyad Khamnei

SATYR is an experimental engineering project aimed at creating a device capable of attenuating acoustic-range sound by using collimated acoustic waves to destructively interfere with an external sound source through the medium of propagation.

The first paper below outlines the experimental basis for collimating acoustic-range sound with parabolic and Fresnel reflectors. Subsequent work is intended to investigate how interference behavior depends on the frequencies, incident angles, and sound pressure levels of both the collimated and external sources.

If the effect can be shown robustly, the next stage is to explore practical use cases for such attenuation devices, including mechanical design constraints, engineering challenges, and possible market applications.

Quantitative Modeling / Sports Analytics Independent / Collaborative Development

Maybank Tennis Algorithm

A quantitative approach to modeling tennis performance and momentum using probabilistic adjustment and sequence-based prediction methods.

Principal Investigator

Christian Kohnle

Collaborators

Stafford Yerger

This project explores a novel approach to tennis performance evaluation using Bayesian-adjusted sequence modeling to estimate a player's current form relative to broader skill level and recent performance history.

The goal is to quantify and track performance momentum in a way that is predictive, interpretable, and useful for downstream modeling. The implementation and application of the system are still under active development.
