Sound Attenuation by Transmission Yeilding Resonators (SATYR)

Principle Investigator:

Christian Kohnle

Co-Investigator(s):

Shahyad Khamnei

Brief Description:

Experimental engineering project that aims to create a device capable of attenuating acoustic-range sound by using collimated acoustic waves to destructively interfere with the medium of propogation.

Papers, Proceedings, and Presentations:

The first paper below outlines the experimental basis for collimating acoustic-range sound with parabolic and Fresnel reflectors.

Following papers will use such acoustic Fresnel reflectors to interfere with external sound sources and determine how the resultant sound depends on the frequencies, incident angle, and SPL of both sources. We expect to show that the SPL of the resultant sound can be decreased for some choices of collimated and external sound parameters, albeit with strong sensetivities.

If this can be shown, the next goal is to explore a few use cases for devices capable of using such acoustic attenuation including their mechanics, engineering challenges, and market advantages.
