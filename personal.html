Statement

What draws me to this work

I am most interested in problems that sit at the boundary between theory, computation, and interpretation: places where the underlying ideas are deep, the mathematics is nontrivial, and the practical challenge is not only solving the problem, but also learning how to think about it clearly.

In physics, that has drawn me toward theoretical cosmology, inference, simulation, and mathematically structured models of complex systems. More broadly, I am interested in developing frameworks that make hard problems easier to explore, whether that means improving the efficiency of a scientific pipeline, building clearer conceptual explanations, or designing tools that let insight scale beyond a single calculation.

I also care deeply about explanation. A large part of what motivates this site is the belief that difficult ideas should not remain locked behind bad communication. I want the public-facing side of my work to do more than simplify; I want it to genuinely teach, clarify, and invite deeper engagement.

Over time, I hope to continue developing both as a researcher and as a communicator: someone who contributes technically, thinks carefully, and can help bridge the distance between advanced ideas and broader understanding.
